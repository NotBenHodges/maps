Mistakes when using maps

1. When creating proportional symbol maps manually, it is tempting to
scale symbols such as circles by the diameter. However, this method
creates an area larger than the area the data represents. The area should
be scaled, not the diameter.

2. It is important to place symbols in the place where their values were
observed. When creating a visualization of various regions, symbols should
be placed in the center of thier respective regions or else the reader
might become confused.
